Why is Merida called "The White City"?

Uncertain origin

The origin of such a peculiar nickname is not certain and there are several versions about its history. Besides knowing it as the "White City", the city has another variation: "The White Merida"; which if you ever visit, is going to be very common for you to hear in locals and tourists.

The first version says that it was named after the color white that predominates in the buildings erected by the Spanish over the ruins of Mayan temples.

From architecture to music

Other of the many versions say that Mérida has the name of "White City" because many of its constructions used limestone typical of the region, which make it stand out even more with the rays of the sun, but also tell what the nickname is attributed to the cleanliness of its streets and the color of the traditional clothing of its inhabitants like the guayaberas in men and hipiles in women.

One of the last versions is that the ex president Adolfo Ruiz Cortines came with Pepe Guizar, famous singer, to visit the city. Inspired by the beautiful landscape, he wrote the homonymous song.
